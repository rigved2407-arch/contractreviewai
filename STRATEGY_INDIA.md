## 3. Sales Channels — India

### Channel 1: Direct Outreach to Firms (highest margin)

**Decision-makers to contact:**
- Managing Partner (for small/mid firms)
- COO / Chief Strategy Officer (for large firms)
- Head of Innovation / Knowledge Management
- Practice Head — Corporate / M&A

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn — search "Partner Corporate M&A India" filter by firm
- Email — follow pattern `firstname.lastname@firmname.com`
- Events — Bombay Bar Association, Delhi High Court Bar Association
- Law school alumni networks — NLS, NALSAR, NUJS, NLU Jodhpur, NLU Delhi
- Industry bodies — SCC Online, Manupatra events, CII legal summits

**Sample outreach message (LinkedIn):**

```
Hi [Name],

I noticed [Firm] does significant M&A/corporate work.
We built an AI tool trained specifically on Indian law
(Companies Act, SEBI, CCI, FEMA, DPDP Act, Income Tax Act)
that automates contract review and due diligence.

Your associates currently spend 3 weeks on a standard DD.
Our tool does it in 3 days — with Indian-law-specific red flag
detection that tools like Kira/Luminance miss entirely.

Would you be open to a 15-min call to see if this fits
your practice?

First deal free. No commitment.
```

### Channel 2: LPO Partnerships (volume)

**Model:** Whitelabel your tool to LPOs. They serve AmLaw firms; your tool makes them 5x faster.

**LPO value prop:**
"Instead of 10 associates reviewing docs, use 2 associates + our AI. Same quality, 80% lower cost. You win more contracts from global firms."

**Revenue split:** LPO pays you ₹50K–₹1L/month per active client

## 5. India-Only Product Features (Sell These)

Every sales conversation must highlight these India-specific advantages:

### 1. "Kira and Luminance don't understand Indian law. We do."
- Section 27 non-compete recognition (void in India)
- DPDP Act 2023 compliance vs GDPR
- Companies Act Section 188 RPT thresholds
- Income Tax Act TDS provisions (Section 40(a)(ia) disallowance)
- GST input tax credit verification
- SEBI SAST takeover code triggers
- CCI merger control thresholds

### 2. "Your data stays in India."
- AWS Mumbai / Azure Central India hosting
- DPDP Act 2023 compliant by default
- No data leaves Indian jurisdiction
- On-premise deployment available

### 3. "You pay in INR. No forex, no international pricing."
- GST invoice provided
- No USD conversion
- Indian bank account / UPI / Razorpay payment

### 4. "Benchmarked on Indian contract formats."
- Tested on Indian SHA, JVA, NDA, SPA formats
- Understands Indian citation styles (AIR, SCC, SCR)
- Indian market clause standards (liability caps, indemnity baskets)

## 6. Objection Handling — Indian Law Firm Context

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "AI tools for law firms are not allowed by Bar Council rules" | "The Bar Council permits AI as a tool, not a replacement. Final legal opinion remains with the lawyer. Same as using SCC Online or Manupatra — we're a research accelerator." |
| "Client confidentiality concerns" | "AES-256 encryption at rest, no third-party data sharing, on-premise deployment available. Your data never leaves India. We sign your NDA." |
| "Our clients won't pay for AI-powered billing" | "This reduces cost, not quality. Instead of billing 200 associate hours at ₹3K/hr, your firm keeps the same quality output at a fraction. Bill at a flat fee instead of hourly — clients prefer this." |
| "We already have our own template/playbook system" | "Our playbook engine imports your existing preferred positions. We don't replace your standards — we automate your team's adherence to them." |
| "₹50K/month is too much" | "Your firm pays 1 associate ₹12L/year + overhead. Our tool at ₹6L/year replaces 50% of that associate's DD work. It's an 8x return." |
| "We want to try before buying" | "Upload 3 contracts — free. Full analysis. No credit card. If you're not impressed, walk away." |
| "Will this replace our junior associates?" | "It replaces the tedious parts — reading 2,000 docs for DD. Your associates focus on negotiations, strategy, and client relationships. It makes them better, not redundant." |
